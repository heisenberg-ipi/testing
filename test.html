A Weekend in Kyoto: Finding Peace in Ancient Temples

Posted by Sarah Johnson | June 20, 2025 | Travel, Japan

Last month, I had the incredible opportunity to spend a weekend in Kyoto, Japan. Known for its well-preserved temples, stunning gardens, and traditional wooden houses, Kyoto offers a peaceful escape from the hustle and bustle of modern life.

I started my journey at Fushimi Inari Shrine, famous for its thousands of vermilion torii gates that create tunnels along the hiking trails. Arriving early in the morning allowed me to avoid the crowds and experience the mystical atmosphere as sunlight filtered through the gates.

The highlight of my trip was definitely Kinkaku-ji (the Golden Pavilion). This Zen Buddhist temple is covered in gold leaf, creating a stunning reflection in the surrounding pond. Despite being a popular tourist destination, there was something profoundly serene about standing at the edge of the water, taking in the harmonious balance between architecture and nature.

For those planning a visit to Kyoto, I recommend staying in a traditional ryokan to complete the experience. The tatami floors, sliding paper doors, and communal hot baths offer an authentic glimpse into Japanese culture that you won't find in modern hotels.

What struck me most about Kyoto was the seamless blend of ancient traditions with contemporary life. Locals in traditional kimonos walk alongside students with smartphones, and centuries-old temples stand in the shadows of modern buildings. It's this beautiful contrast that makes Kyoto not just a destination, but an experience to be savored.
